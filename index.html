Kenta's Antics


Hi! My name is Kenta Suzuki, and I am a fourth year undergraduate studying mathematics at MIT. In particular, I am interested in representations of \(p\)-adic groups and geometric representation theory. In my free time, you can find me mixing up spatulas and ladles in Deutsches Haus, running along the Charles River, or drinking tea in the math lounge.

Email: kjsuzuki [at] mit [dot] edu

Other Activities

  • I am co-organizing the STAGE seminar this semester on algebraic differential equations. I also co-organized the seminar in Spring 2023 on Shimura varieties.
  • For the MIT PRIMES program, I am a mentor conducting research with a high school student studying the computation of affine Kazhdan-Lusztig polynomials.
